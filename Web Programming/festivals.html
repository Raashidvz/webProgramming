Festivals In India

ONAM

The Legend of King Mahabali

Onam is associated with the story of the Asura (demon) King Mahabali, a virtuous and benevolent ruler under whom the people of Kerala lived in peace and prosperity. The gods, threatened by his popularity and power, sought help from Lord Vishnu. Vishnu took the avatar of Vamana, a dwarf Brahmin, and approached Mahabali during a ritual. Vamana asked for three steps of land, a request the king generously granted despite a warning from his guru. Vamana then grew to a colossal size and covered the earth and the heavens in two steps. For the third step, Mahabali humbly offered his own head, an act of supreme devotion. Vishnu sent Mahabali to the netherworld but, pleased with his kindness, granted him the boon to visit his people once a year. Onam celebrates this annual homecoming.

Holi

Holi is an ancient Hindu festival of colors that celebrates the victory of good over evil, the arrival of spring, and the power of love and new beginnings. Celebrated predominantly in India and Nepal, its joyous traditions have spread worldwide, bringing people together from all walks of life. The festival lasts for a night and a day, beginning on the last full moon day of the Hindu lunar month of Phalguna, which typically falls in March.

Diwali

Diwali, the "Festival of Lights," is a major five-day festival celebrated across India and by people of Indian descent globally. It symbolizes the victory of good over evil, light over darkness, and knowledge over ignorance, with different communities and religions like Hinduism, Jainism, and Sikhism observing their own significant traditions. The festival features decorations like clay lamps (diyas) and colorful rangoli, feasting, family gatherings, the exchange of gifts, and the worship of deities like Goddess Lakshmi for prosperity.
